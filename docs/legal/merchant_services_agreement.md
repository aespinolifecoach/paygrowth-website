1.3 **"Applicable Law"** means all federal, state, and local laws, statutes, regulations, rules, codes, ordinances, and orders of any government authority applicable to the Services, including but not limited to: (a) The Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) and Regulation E; (b) The Truth in Lending Act (TILA) and Regulation Z; (c) The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) and Regulation P; (d) State money transmitter laws; (e) Anti-money laundering (AML) laws including the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and USA PATRIOT Act; (f) The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS).

1.4 **"Business Day"** means Monday through Friday, excluding federal banking holidays.

1.7 **"Chargeback"** means a transaction that is disputed by a cardholder and subsequently reversed through the Card Networks.

1.8 **"Effective Date"** means the date this Agreement is executed by both parties or the date Merchant first processes a transaction through Processor's systems, whichever occurs first.

1.9 **"Equipment"** means any POS terminals, card readers, PIN pads, or other hardware provided by Processor to Merchant in connection with the Services.

1.10 **"Fees"** means all charges, fees, assessments, interchange fees, dues, and surcharges described in the Fee Schedule (Exhibit A) and incurred by Merchant in connection with the Services.

1.11 **"Funds"** means settlement amounts due to Merchant from payment card transactions processed through the Services.

1.12 **"PCI DSS"** means the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard, as amended from time to time by the PCI Security Standards Council.

1.13 **"Reserve"** means funds held by Processor as security for Chargebacks, refunds, fees, or other liabilities under this Agreement.

1.14 **"Services"** means the payment processing services, gateway services, equipment, reporting, and other products described in this Agreement and any exhibits.

1.15 **"Settlement"** means the transfer of net transaction proceeds from Processor to Merchant's designated depository account.

1.16 **"Sponsored Bank"** or **"Acquiring Bank"** means the financial institution that sponsors Processor's ISO activities and through which transactions are settled.

1.17 **"Transaction"** means each individual payment card transaction, ACH transaction, or other payment processed through the Services.

## 2. SERVICES

### 2.1 Scope of Services
Processor agrees to provide Merchant with payment processing services as described in this Agreement. Services may include: (a) Credit card processing (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover); (b) Debit card processing (PIN-based and signature-based); (c) ACH processing; (d) Payment gateway services; (e) Virtual terminal services; (f) Recurring billing and subscription management; (g) Chargeback management and representment; (h) Fraud prevention tools (AVS, CVV2 verification, 3D Secure); (i) Reporting and analytics; (j) Equipment provisioning and support.

### 2.2 Service Levels
Processor will use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain: (a) Gateway uptime of at least 99.5% per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance; (b) Transaction processing availability 24/7/365; (c) Customer support response within 4 hours for critical issues during Business Hours; (d) Settlement within 1-3 Business Days of transaction processing.

### 2.3 Changes to Services
Processor reserves the right to modify, suspend, or discontinue any aspect of the Services at any time with 30 days' prior written notice to Merchant, provided that such changes do not materially adversely affect Merchant's processing capabilities.

3.2 **Business Type**: Merchant's business type, products, and services are accurately described in the application and no aspect of Merchant's business is prohibited under Card Network rules or Applicable Law.

## 4. FEES AND SETTLEMENT

### 4.1 Fees
Merchant agrees to pay all Fees as set forth in the Fee Schedule attached as Exhibit A, as amended from time to time. Fees include but are not limited to: discount rates and transaction fees; monthly minimum fees; statement fees; PCI compliance fees; chargeback fees; annual or setup fees; equipment fees or lease payments; gateway or monthly service fees; ACH processing fees; AVS fees; batch settlement fees.

### 4.2 Fee Changes
Processor may change Fees upon 30 days' prior written notice. Fee changes resulting from changes in interchange rates, Card Network assessments, or regulatory requirements may take effect immediately upon such change.

### 4.3 Settlement
Processor will settle net transaction proceeds (gross transaction amounts less applicable Fees, Chargebacks, refunds, and reserves) to Merchant's designated depository account in accordance with standard settlement schedules, typically within 1-3 Business Days.

### 4.4 Settlement Delay
Processor reserves the right to delay settlement if: (a) Processor reasonably suspects fraudulent or suspicious activity; (b) Merchant exceeds risk thresholds established by Processor; (c) Required by Applicable Law or Card Network rules; (d) Merchant fails to provide required documentation.

### 4.5 Reserve
Processor may require Merchant to maintain a Reserve in an amount determined by Processor based on risk assessment. Processor may fund the Reserve by withholding a percentage of each Settlement. The Reserve will be held for a period determined by Processor to cover Chargebacks, refunds, fees, and other liabilities.

## 5. CHARGEBACKS

5.1 **Liability**: Merchant is solely liable for all Chargebacks incurred in connection with transactions processed under this Agreement.

5.2 **Chargeback Fees**: Merchant agrees to pay the Chargeback fee set forth in Exhibit A for each Chargeback received.

5.3 **Chargeback Threshold**: If Merchant's Chargeback ratio exceeds Card Network thresholds (currently 1% of monthly transactions or 100 Chargebacks per month for Visa; 1.5% for Mastercard), Processor may: (a) Impose additional reserves; (b) Increase Chargeback fees; (c) Require a remediation plan; (d) Suspend or terminate Services.

5.4 **Representment**: Processor may, in its discretion, attempt representment of Chargebacks on Merchant's behalf. Merchant must provide all requested documentation within 5 Business Days of a Chargeback notice.

---

## 6. PCI DSS COMPLIANCE

6.1 **Obligation**: Merchant must at all times comply with PCI DSS requirements applicable to its processing volume (as set forth in the PCI DSS Compliance Guide provided by Processor).

6.2 **Validation**: Merchant must submit annual PCI DSS validation (SAQ or report on compliance) as required by the PCI Security Standards Council and provide evidence of compliance to Processor upon request.

6.3 **Non-Compliance**: If Merchant fails to maintain PCI DSS compliance, Processor may: (a) Assess non-compliance fees; (b) Pass through any Card Network non-compliance fines or assessments; (c) Suspend or terminate Services.

6.4 **Data Security**: Merchant agrees not to store full magnetic stripe data, CVV2/CVC2 codes, or PIN data after transaction authorization. Merchant must protect stored cardholder data in accordance with PCI DSS requirements.

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Term
This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of three (3) years (the "Initial Term"), and automatically renews for successive one (1) year periods unless either party gives at least 60 days' written notice of non-renewal.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if: (a) The other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 15 days of written notice; (b) The other party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases operations; (c) Required by Card Network rules or Applicable Law; (d) Merchant's processing volume drops below 50% of projected monthly volume for three consecutive months.

### 7.3 Termination Without Cause
Merchant may terminate this Agreement without cause upon 30 days' written notice, subject to payment of any early termination fees described in Exhibit A.

### 7.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination: (a) All outstanding Fees become immediately due; (b) Processor will settle all uncontested Funds due to Merchant; (c) Merchant must immediately cease using the Services; (d) Merchant must return or destroy all Equipment as directed by Processor; (e) Sections governing confidentiality, indemnification, limitation of liability, and survival will continue.
